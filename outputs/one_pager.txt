CAPITAL GROUP SOCIAL MEDIA POST GENERATION — CASE STUDY
Kia Rahmani  |  2026-05-20

PROBLEM
For each of 16 Capital Group article URLs, draft 3-5 candidate social
media posts (LinkedIn / X) that a marketing team member can review,
edit, and publish. The training data is 279 historical (post, article)
pairs; no labels exist for the test set.

APPROACH
Two-stage pipeline.

Pre-generation (one-time):
  - Deterministic style audit of the train corpus (length, hashtag,
    URL, disclosure-trailer, emoji rates), stratified by audience
    track. Confirms 0% emoji usage, sharp per-track style differences,
    one canonical disclosure URL (bit.ly/2JzEDWl, 180 occurrences).
  - Article body acquisition. Live capitalgroup.com URLs are
    auth-walled by Akamai; bodies sourced from Wayback Machine
    snapshots via year-fallback (2024 → 2023 → 2025). 16/16 recovered.
  - Voyage 3.5 embeddings of 279 train postOriginals (as documents)
    and 16 test article bodies (as queries). Asymmetric document/query
    encoding for retrieval quality. URLs and disclosure trailers
    stripped before embedding to surface topic signal over boilerplate.

Generation (per test article, per variant):
  - Retrieve top-8 train posts via cosine similarity. Hard filter on
    audience track (advisor/institutional/content). Dedupe by URL
    (train has 134 unique URLs across 279 rows, so many paraphrase
    pairs — dedupe ensures we surface 8 distinct articles, not 8
    paraphrases of the same one).
  - Prompt: system constraints (no emojis, no return claims, no
    buy/sell, no tickers) + retrieved examples + article title/URL/body
    + per-variant style direction. Cache breakpoints on system and on
    examples-plus-article block; per-variant delta is uncached.
  - Variant diversity via round-robin flavor tags: question_hook,
    stat_lead, contrarian, theme_summary (4 of 6 configured). Each
    flavor is a one-line style direction injected into the uncached
    delta.
  - Inline quality gate: programmatic length check (100-350 chars)
    plus an LLM judge using Claude tool-use for forced structured
    output. Judge scores voice/topic/hook (1-5) and compliance
    (pass/fail) with notes. Hard-gate failure (length or compliance)
    triggers retry with feedback injected into the uncached delta,
    max 2 retries; cached chunks preserved across retries.
  - Judge calibrated via 5 few-shot examples in config covering
    positive cases and the four compliance trigger types (return
    claims, buy/sell verbs, guarantees, single-ticker mentions).

FINDINGS

  Held-out evaluation on 10 URLs (7 advisor / 2 institutional /
  1 content, biased toward URLs with 3+ paraphrase pairs in the train
  corpus). For each held-out URL, removed all paraphrases from the
  retrieval pool, generated 4 variants using slug-title query +
  remaining train as examples, compared against the URL's real-post
  cluster.

  Embedding similarity (cosine in Voyage 3.5 space):
    - gen vs best-real (avg across 40 gen posts):  0.814
    - inter-human ceiling (avg pairwise cosine
      within multi-paraphrase clusters):           0.811
    - Gap:                                          -0.003

  Generated posts land at the inter-human ceiling. The pipeline
  produces posts as similar to a real Capital Group post as Capital
  Group's own writers' posts are to each other. Per-cluster signal is
  mixed — on some URLs we sit above ceiling (possible mode collapse
  to a common safe template), on others below (humans more cohesive).
  Aggregated: parity.

  Judge scores (Claude Sonnet 4.6 judge applied to both):
    - voice_match:           gen 4.67   vs   real 3.80    gap +0.88
    - on_topic:              gen 4.17   vs   real 3.14    gap +1.03
    - hook_strength:         gen 3.67   vs   real 2.97    gap +0.70
    - compliance failures:   gen 0/40   vs   real 2/35

  Generated posts score above the human reference on all three soft
  rubrics. Three honest interpretations:

    (1) Judge bias. The judge sees the same retrieved examples the
        generator used. It may reward gen for matching them.

    (2) Human variance. Real corpus posts span the full quality range
        (in the eval slice, some real posts score 2/1/1 on this
        rubric). Generated posts cluster tighter and safer. Two of
        the 35 held-out real posts even fail compliance per the same
        judge — the corpus average is not uniformly strong.

    (3) Genuine pipeline quality. Possibly the pipeline produces more
        consistent marketing copy than the human corpus average,
        because it combines a tuned system prompt + retrieved
        exemplars + compliance gate + flavor diversity in one pass.

  All three are partly true. The strongest defensible read: the
  pipeline produces posts that are statistically indistinguishable
  from the human corpus on embedding similarity, and judged at or
  above the human baseline by a Sonnet evaluator on voice / topic /
  hook quality, with a perfect compliance record.

  Submission run quality gate behavior (16 articles × 4 variants
  with --flavors all, Claude Sonnet 4.6):
    - Posts produced:                 64
    - Compliance failures (final):    0/64
    - Compliance failures (any
      attempt across 67 calls):       0
    - Length envelope adherence:      64/64 in 100-350 chars
                                      (range 147-342, median 261)
    - Retries triggered:              2/64 (resolved on attempt 2)
    - Posts flagged out of budget:    0/64
    - Cache hit rate on judge calls:  16/16 articles engaged
                                      (Sonnet's 1024-token minimum
                                      vs Haiku's 2048)
    - Total cost (gen + judge):       $0.75

LIMITATIONS
  - Article body sourced from Wayback, not live API. Capital Group
    auth-walled the article corpus since the case study was authored;
    in production this is solved with an internal API or
    session-cookie passthrough. Wayback is also non-deterministic
    across runs — the committed article cache pins the specific
    snapshots used in the submission (each cache file records its
    source: "wayback:<timestamp>"). The fetch script is idempotent
    (skip-if-exists; --force to rebuild) so re-running does not
    corrupt the submission cache.
  - Institutional track is sparse (34 train posts vs 231 advisor).
    Retrieval signal for institutional articles weaker (top similarity
    ~0.5 vs ~0.7 on advisor). No cross-track fallback in v1.
  - Eval uses slug-title only (no body) for held-out URL queries.
    Judge's on_topic score for both gen and real posts is computed
    without article-body context, so the absolute on_topic number is
    less informative than the gen-vs-real gap.
  - Judge bias risk: the judge sees the same retrieval examples that
    gen used. May over-reward gen for matching those examples. The
    favorable gen-vs-real judge gap should be read with this caveat.
    Mitigation deferred (would require an independent example set or
    different model).
  - No URL shortener for the bit.ly convention dominant in advisor
    posts (74% of advisor train posts use bit.ly). Generated posts
    use the full capitalgroup.com URL. Cosmetic.
  - Post-hoc regex sanity grep on the final output confirmed: 0
    return-claim patterns, 0 buy/sell verbs, 0 guarantee language,
    0 ticker mentions across all 64 generated variants.

WHAT I'D DO WITH MORE TIME
  - HyDE-style query expansion for held-out / sparse-track retrieval.
    Use the LLM to synthesize an ideal hypothetical post for the
    article, embed that, retrieve. Better matching when the query
    is short or stylistically out-of-distribution.
  - Cross-track retrieval fallback for sparse tracks: if institutional
    top-k similarity falls below threshold, augment with advisor
    posts and mark them as cross-track in the prompt.
  - Judge debiasing: re-judge with a different retrieval pool, or
    swap in a different frontier model (GPT-5, Gemini 2.5) as the
    judge. Tests whether the gen-favors gap survives independent
    evaluation.
  - Live A/B in the marketing workflow: ship a small batch to the
    team, measure approve/edit/reject rates vs the existing process.
