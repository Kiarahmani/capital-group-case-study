CAPITAL GROUP SOCIAL MEDIA POST GENERATION — CASE STUDY
Kia Rahmani  |  2026-05-19

PROBLEM
For each of 16 Capital Group article URLs, draft 3-5 candidate social
media posts (LinkedIn / X) that a marketing team member can review,
edit, and publish. The training data is 279 historical (post, article)
pairs; no labels exist for the test set.

APPROACH
Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) pipeline in two stages.

Pre-generation (one-time):
  - Deterministic style audit of the train corpus (length, hashtag,
    URL, disclosure-trailer rates), stratified by audience track.
    Surfaces sharp per-track style differences and one canonical
    disclosure URL (bit.ly/2JzEDWl, 180 occurrences).
  - Audience tracks. URL-path-based groupings of the train corpus —
    `/advisor/insights/` (231 posts, advisor track),
    `/institutional/insights/` (34, institutional), and
    `/content/capital-group/` (14, content) — corresponding to who
    each marketing post is written for. Hashtag rate, URL convention,
    and disclosure-trailer frequency differ meaningfully across tracks.
  - Article body acquisition. Many capitalgroup.com URLs in the
    provided train and test files point to content that has since
    been moved or removed; the live URL lands on a generic Capital Group 
    landing page with no article body. Article bodies are recovered from 
    Wayback Machine snapshots via year-fallback (2024 → 2023 → 2025). 
  - Voyage 3.5 embeddings of 279 train postOriginals and 16 test
    article bodies. Voyage exposes separate `document` and `query`
    encoding modes that produce different vectors for the same text
    depending on its role in retrieval; using both modes (train posts
    as documents, test articles as queries) is documented best
    practice and improves retrieval quality. URLs and disclosure
    trailers are stripped before embedding to surface topic signal
    over boilerplate.

Generation (RAG, per test article, per variant):
  - Retrieve top-8 train posts via cosine similarity over the Voyage
    embedding space. Hard filter on audience track. Dedupe by URL
    (train has 134 unique URLs across 279 rows, so many paraphrase
    pairs — dedupe ensures 8 distinct articles as examples, not 8
    paraphrases of the same one).
  - Prompt: system constraints (compliance + style) + retrieved
    examples + article title/URL/body + per-variant style direction.
    The system prompt and the examples-plus-article block sit behind
    a cache breakpoint and are reused across the N variants for the
    same article; only the small per-variant instruction (style
    direction or retry feedback) sits outside the cache, so each
    variant reuses ~95% of input tokens at the cached rate.
  - Variant diversity via round-robin flavor tags (e.g., question_hook,
    stat_lead, contrarian, theme_summary). Each
    flavor is a one-line style direction.
  - Inline quality gate: three hard gates plus three soft scores per
    variant. Hard gates trigger retry with targeted feedback:
      (1) Programmatic length check (100-350 chars).
      (2) Compliance check (LLM judge, pass/fail): no specific
          return claims, no buy/sell verbs, no guarantee language,
          no single-ticker mentions.
      (3) Output-shape check (LLM judge, pass/fail): is this a
          valid, helpful publishable post directed at the reader?
    Soft scores (voice_match, on_topic, hook_strength, 1-5 each) are
    reported but never trigger retries. Judge uses Claude tool-use
    for forced structured JSON output. Hard-gate failure triggers
    retry with feedback injected into the uncached delta; max 2
    retries; best-attempt selection if budget exhausted. Judge
    calibrated with few-shot examples in config covering compliance
    triggers and output-shape failure modes.

FINDINGS
Held-out evaluation on 10 train URLs (7 advisor / 2 institutional /
1 content), preferring URLs that have 3+ human-written posts in
train so each generated post can be compared against a real-post
cluster rather than a single reference. For each held-out URL, all
its real posts are removed from the retrieval pool, the article
body is fetched fresh from Wayback, 4 variants are generated, and
each generated post is scored against the URL's real-post cluster.

  Embedding similarity (cosine in Voyage 3.5 space):
    - gen_vs_best_real (cosine of each generated post to its single
      closest match in the URL's real-post cluster, avg over 40
      generated posts):                                       0.817
    - inter-human ceiling (avg pairwise cosine between real posts
      within each multi-paraphrase cluster — how similar real
      Capital Group writers are to each other when writing about
      the same article):                                      0.811
    - Gap:                                                    +0.006

    Generated posts sit at the inter-human ceiling on aggregate (gap
    within 1pp).

  Judge scores (Claude Sonnet 4.6 applied to both gen and real, with
  article body in scope for both):
    - voice_match:           gen 4.67   vs   real 3.51
    - on_topic:              gen 4.88   vs   real 3.49
    - hook_strength:         gen 3.90   vs   real 2.77
    - compliance failures:   gen 0/40   vs   real 0/35
    - is_valid_post failures: gen 0/40  vs   real 0/35

    Generated posts score above the human reference on all three
    soft rubrics. Caveat: the judge scores voice_match against the
    same 8 retrieved examples the generator used as style reference.
    Gen was optimized to match them; real held-out posts never saw
    them. Some of the gap reflects this artifact, not pure quality.
    Detailed per-cluster numbers and per-post judge notes are in
    outputs/eval_*.md and outputs/eval_*.csv.

  Submission run (16 articles × 4 variants, Claude Sonnet 4.6,
  --flavors all): 64 posts produced, 0 compliance failures, 0
  is_valid_post failures, all in length envelope (142-345 chars,
  median 265), 2 retries triggered (both resolved on attempt 2),
  0 posts flagged out of retry budget. Total cost $0.91. The
  is_valid_post gate caught one meta-commentary preamble on the
  submission run that would have passed compliance regex; clean
  post produced on retry.

WHAT I'D DO WITH MORE TIME
Ship a small batch to the marketing team and measure
approve / edit / reject rates against their existing process.
Soft judge scores are defensible methodologically but only a
live workflow A/B answers "are these posts actually publishable
as-is, or just good-looking."

STACK
  - Python 3.11, uv (packaging).
  - Claude Sonnet 4.6 (gen + judge) via the Anthropic SDK, with
    prompt caching for the shared cached chunk and tool-use for
    forced JSON-structured judge output.
  - Voyage 3.5 for embeddings (document/query asymmetric, as
    described above).
  - trafilatura for article body extraction from HTML.
  - pandas for table I/O (xlsx in, csv/xlsx out).
  - typer for the two CLI entry points (capgroup-gen, capgroup-eval).
  - openpyxl as pandas' .xlsx backend.

Repo: github.com/Kiarahmani/capital-group-case-study.

OUTPUT BUNDLE
  - posts.csv / posts.xlsx — long-format: postId, variantId, flavor,
    audienceTrack, articleUrl, generatedPost, voiceMatch, onTopic,
    hookStrength, compliance, complianceNotes, isValidPost,
    isValidPostNotes, lengthChars, judgeRetries, judgeFlagged.
  - trace.jsonl — per-call provenance: retrieved example postIds and
    similarities, all retry attempts with judge notes, token usage.
  - eval.csv / eval_summary.md — held-out evaluation findings
    (per-post metrics and aggregate summary).
